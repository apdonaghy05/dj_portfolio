Expectancy Sheet

This Expectancy Sheet was created for my 7th grade ELA classes at Billy Lane Lauffer Middle School. It continues to represent my classroom expectations and procedures and is updated to reflect any changes to district-wide rules and policies.

Our 7th grade “Choices and Consequences” Unit contains a benchmark project that engages students in the study and presentation of a historical figure that embodies truth and liberty in their pursuit of justice. As often as possible each student’s unique discourse is promoted in the classroom through the integration of culturally relevant content.

As displayed in my Expectancy Sheet “Letter Home” section, building trust with students’ parents and guardians is key to a successful academic relationship. The better understanding promoted between myself and students’ families, within the district community, among fellow faculty, and with the students themselves, the better I can work to implement effective learning goals, teaching strategies, and ultimately support each student throughout the school year. (InTASC Standards 1, 2, 10)
